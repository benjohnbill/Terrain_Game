# Terrain Game — English Name Screening

> **Layer:** Working (`.context/`)  
> **Date:** 2026-07-16  
> **Purpose:** Preliminary market-facing name exploration for the game currently called `Terrain_Game`. This note is non-normative and does not rename the project.

> **User decision — 2026-07-16:** `Strategy Ground` was selected as the
> provisional market-facing working title. It remains subject to complete
> name/Hero testing and later collision, trademark, domain, and handle checks.

## 1. Confirmed Naming Brief

The candidate name should:

- center the fantasy of a player **authoring a decisive war through accumulated judgments**;
- treat terrain as a supporting mechanism rather than the entire product identity;
- use existing English words, not a coined word;
- preferably contain one or two words;
- read as a noun or noun phrase rather than an imperative or sentence;
- work as a global Steam-facing title with a serious strategy tone;
- remain reasonably pronounceable for Korean speakers;
- leave room for the fictional world, emergent history, and later product expansion.

The name does not need to carry every product promise. The English Hero and subheadline can explain compressed playtime, low micromanagement, and the fictional setting.

## 2. Method and Limits

### Sources used

- Current Steam catalog queries through Valve's official Store Search API, checked on 2026-07-16.
- Current Steam product pages where an exact or confusingly similar title appeared.
- First-party U.S. Army and U.S. Marine Corps material for the meaning of military-doctrine candidates.
- Current official WIPO and USPTO guidance for the limits of preliminary trademark searching.

### Important limits

- A Steam search returning no exact title is **not evidence that a name is legally or commercially available**. It is only a point-in-time store-catalog screen.
- Steam search is fuzzy. The assessment records whether an exact or confusingly similar title appears among the returned products, not merely the raw result count.
- No legal trademark clearance was performed. Goods/services classes, target filing jurisdictions, common-law use, company names, domain names, and phonetic/semantic similarity still require a later clearance pass.
- The official USPTO interface is interactive, and a useful search requires similar-mark and coordinated-class analysis. WIPO likewise recommends searching both its Global Brand Database and relevant national/regional registers before filing. See [USPTO Trademark Search](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/search) and [WIPO search-before-filing guidance](https://www.wipo.int/en/web/madrid-system/how_to/search/index).
- Hero lines below are pairing probes, not final copy.

## 4. Ranked Shortlist

### 1. Chosen Ground

- **Semantic fit:** Excellent. It suggests that the commander chooses where and under what conditions the decisive contest occurs. It joins player agency and terrain without making terrain the whole product.
- **Korean reading:** `초즌 그라운드` — easy and stable.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Chosen%20Ground&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact Steam title on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Medium. The exact phrase is used outside games, including historical interpretation and tabletop scenario language, so it is not an empty phrase-space.
- **Main risk:** It can imply that players literally construct or select the terrain. Product copy must clarify that players read geography and shape the conditions of battle on it.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `Choose the ground. Shape the war.`
- **Verdict:** Strongest balance of system truth, seriousness, pronunciation, and title compactness.

### 2. Unwritten Wars

- **Semantic fit:** Excellent for the emotional promise. The player produces wars that resemble historical turning points without reenacting scripted history.
- **Korean reading:** `언리튼 워즈` — easy.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Unwritten%20Wars&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact Steam title on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Medium-high within the Steam game-title space at this snapshot.
- **Main risk:** It sounds more like an anthology, narrative game, or alternate-history collection than a systems-heavy strategy simulation. The Hero must supply the strategy genre and player action.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `No scripts. No reenactments. Only the wars your decisions create.`
- **Verdict:** Best expression of emergent history and player-authored war.

### 3. Grand Gambit

- **Semantic fit:** Strong. It frames a match as a large-scale plan in which ground, strength, time, or information may be risked to create a decisive payoff.
- **Korean reading:** `그랜드 갬빗` — easy and memorable.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Grand%20Gambit&l=english&cc=us) returned `Grandmaster's Gambit`, but no exact `Grand Gambit` title, on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Medium. `Gambit` is heavily used across chess, card games, films, books, and game subtitles.
- **Main risk:** It can make the product sound like a chess-inspired board or card game, and a gambit specifically implies a sacrifice rather than the full range of war planning.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `Risk ground. Hide strength. Win the war you planned.`
- **Verdict:** Strongest compact brand among the planning/sacrifice candidates, but less exact than `Chosen Ground`.

### 4. Main Effort

- **Semantic fit:** Strong. U.S. Marine Corps doctrine describes the main effort as the action most critical to success and the focal point where combat power converges. That mirrors the game's allocation of judgment and resources toward a decisive result. See [MCDP 1-3, Tactics](https://www.marines.mil/portals/1/publications/mcdp%201-3%20tactics.pdf).
- **Korean reading:** `메인 에포트` — easy.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Main%20Effort&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact Steam title on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Low-medium. It is a standard military and organizational phrase, which weakens distinctiveness and makes general search results noisy.
- **Main risk:** It may sound like a scenario module, doctrine manual, or military organization rather than a fictional strategy game.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `Every move supports the one that decides the war.`
- **Verdict:** Semantically rigorous and serious, but commercially dry.

### 5. Decisive Stroke

- **Semantic fit:** Strong for the payoff. It communicates that several preparations culminate in one war-changing action.
- **Korean reading:** `디사이시브 스트로크` — understandable but long.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Decisive%20Stroke&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact Steam title on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Medium.
- **Main risk:** `Stroke` also strongly evokes medicine, painting, swimming, and golf. The title overemphasizes the finishing blow and underrepresents the preceding judgments.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `Prepare across turns. Strike once. Change the war.`
- **Verdict:** Forceful, but secondary meanings and pronunciation reduce its rank.

### 6. Decisive Point

- **Semantic fit:** Excellent. Army doctrine describes the decisive point as the terrain-, enemy-, or time-oriented point at which combined combat power can make mission success irreversible. This closely matches the game's accumulating choices and decisive-battle spine. See the [U.S. Army Infantry doctrine supplement](https://www.benning.army.mil/Infantry/DoctrineSupplement/ATP3-21.8/appendix_a/StepsofTroopLeadingProcedures/Step3/index.html).
- **Korean reading:** `디사이시브 포인트` — clear but long.
- **Steam screen:** [Exact query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Decisive%20Point&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact title. However, Steam currently lists [Decision Point: Battlegroup Operational Simulator](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3824590/), a confusingly similar title in the same operational-wargame market.
- **Searchability:** Low-medium because it is a standard doctrinal phrase and the U.S. Army also uses `Decisive Point` as a publication/podcast label.
- **Main risk:** The same-market proximity to `Decision Point` is substantial even without an exact collision.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `Shape the moment that decides the war.`
- **Verdict:** One of the most exact semantic matches, but probably too close to an existing Steam wargame title.

### 7. Unwritten Front

- **Semantic fit:** Good. It combines emergent history with a recognizably military noun and avoids suggesting historical reenactment.
- **Korean reading:** `언리튼 프런트` — easy.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Unwritten%20Front&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact Steam title on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Medium-high within the Steam title space at this snapshot.
- **Main risk:** `Front` can narrow expectations toward a continuous frontline or tactical front-management game. The phrase is also less idiomatic than `Unwritten Wars`.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `Every front has a history. Your decisions write this one.`
- **Verdict:** A workable compromise, but weaker English rhythm than the top three.

### 8. Generalship

- **Semantic fit:** Good. It names the player's actual skill: judgment in planning and directing military operations rather than mechanical execution.
- **Korean reading:** `제너럴십` — pronounceable, though unfamiliar to many Korean players.
- **Steam screen:** [Official query](https://store.steampowered.com/api/storesearch/?term=Generalship&l=english&cc=us) returned no exact Steam title on 2026-07-16.
- **Searchability:** Medium. It is a real but generic English noun used heavily in military history and leadership writing.
- **Main risk:** It lacks the fictional-war and emergent-history dimensions, and some players may not know the word immediately.
- **Hero pairing probe:** `A war of judgment, not busywork.`
- **Verdict:** Best one-word real-English candidate found, but not as evocative or accessible as the top noun phrases.

## 7. Recommendation for the Next Naming Round

Advance three deliberately different candidates rather than many near-synonyms:

1. **`Chosen Ground`** — the system-led candidate;
2. **`Unwritten Wars`** — the player-fantasy-led candidate;
3. **`Grand Gambit`** — the compact strategic-brand candidate.

Test each as a complete lockup with:

- a Steam capsule-scale wordmark;
- one English Hero line;
- one plain-English genre descriptor;
- a 10-second explanation given to a strategy player who has not seen the project.

Suggested descriptor held constant for the comparison:

> `A compressed turn-based war simulation where terrain, information, and a handful of decisions build toward battles no script could write.`

Before any name is adopted publicly, perform a separate clearance pass covering WIPO, USPTO, EUIPO/TMview, KIPO, company and common-law usage, domains/social handles, and phonetic similarity. WIPO explicitly warns that its multi-register database should be supplemented with relevant national and regional registers; a zero-result store search cannot replace that work.
