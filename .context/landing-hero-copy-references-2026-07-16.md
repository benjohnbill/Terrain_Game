## Scope and method

- Inspected on 2026-07-16.
- Six live official product pages: three large publisher/studio games and three indie or small-studio games.
- Official product sites were preferred over store listings.
- On pages where the visual hero contains only a logo and CTA, the first explanatory block immediately below it is recorded separately.
- Quoted copy is deliberately short. Page wording and layout can change after the inspection date.

### Large-game pattern

The large games do not attempt to summarize all mechanics in the hero. They first assign a role through a direct verb—`Build`, `Steer`, `Take charge`, or `Be`—and let known IP, key art, trailers, and extensive proof sections carry the rest.

That economy is not directly transferable to an unknown game. The franchise name already answers “why should I care?” for these pages. A new Terrain Game page needs a short player fantasy **and** a clarifying line that identifies the distinctive experience.

### Indie pattern

The strongest indie pages quickly sell one of three things:

1. **Large fantasy plus removed burden** — Slipways.
2. **A specific combination of experiences** — Manor Lords.
3. **Approachability backed by existing proof** — Polytopia.

Slipways is the closest copy-structure analogue for Terrain Game. It names the genre-scale desire and neutralizes the expected time/micromanagement burden without sounding like a design document.

## Implications for Terrain Game

### Why the previous line felt weak

“승리는 전투에서 만들어지지 않는다. 그 전에 설계된다.” is an aphorism. It describes a belief about war, but it does not yet:

- give the visitor an active role;
- name the unusual outcome they can create;
- distinguish Terrain Game from other strategy games;
- resolve the burden of long campaigns or excessive micromanagement;
- offer immediate visual or interactive proof.

The observed pages suggest that the hero should not lead with a design principle alone. `High Complexity, Low Micromanagement` belongs as supporting explanation or proof unless transformed into a concrete player benefit.

### Recommended message architecture to test

1. **Headline:** a player action or identity paired with an extraordinary war outcome.
2. **Subheadline:** a concrete explanation—terrain-led turn-based war design, compressed into one match, with few high-consequence decisions.
3. **Primary CTA:** honest for the current stage, such as seeing the war-design experience rather than implying an available build.
4. **Hero proof:** an actual map state, short gameplay sequence, or interactive decision chain. Terrain Game currently lacks awards and franchise recognition, so product evidence must perform the trust-building role.

The first copy territory worth testing is therefore:

> **player action + history-worthy emergent outcome + reduced time/control burden**

This is a working copy formula, not approved copy. Exact claims about match duration should remain provisional until playtest evidence supports them.
