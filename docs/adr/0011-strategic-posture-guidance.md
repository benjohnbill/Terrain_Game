# ADR 0011: Strategic Posture as Guidance

Date: 2026-06-29

Status: Accepted

## Context

The game needs to help players reason about four action capacities such as
command, administration, diplomacy, and scholarship/technology. Province
ownership, population, function, and national traits can change those
capacities, but the player should not be forced into repetitive micromanagement.

The user wants presets that clarify strategic direction without becoming
one-click automatic bonuses. Extreme presets should be readable as risky
national commitments, not neutral always-good modes.

## Decision

Use strategic posture as a guidance and planning system, not as a direct bonus
mode.

Strategic postures may include examples such as:

- militarist push;
- administrative consolidation;
- diplomatic stabilization;
- scholarly investment;
- frontier defense;
- economic recovery.

Postures should help the player understand:

- which action capacities are emphasized;
- which actions become easier to prioritize;
- which opportunity costs are being accepted;
- which map targets or defensive fronts become more relevant;
- why an extreme commitment may create long-term risk.

Selecting a posture should not automatically grant hidden bonuses or force a
complete build path. It should guide allocation of the four action capacities
and allow the player to fine-tune values or choices.

## UX Principle

The desired interaction is analysis-heavy but click-light.

The player may spend time reading the situation and planning, but routine
turn execution should stay low-micromanagement. As a working guideline, a
normal turn should aim to require no more than about three major player
confirmations after the player has chosen their direction.

More detailed adjustment can exist for higher difficulty or advanced play, but
the default experience should emphasize strategic analysis over repetitive
manual execution.

## Consequences

Strategic postures can provide help text, previews, warnings, and suggested
targets without taking agency away from the player.

Extreme postures should communicate "this is a risky concentrated strategy"
rather than presenting themselves as balanced presets.

UI should show how province acquisition or strategic decisions affect command,
administration, diplomacy, and scholarship/technology capacity.
