# ADR 0001: Terrain-First East Asia-Inspired World

Date: 2026-06-29

Status: Accepted

## Context

The original prototype is a world-conquest hex game. The desired direction is a
deeper national management and conquest game with a Civilization-like feeling.

The player wants the core fun to remain map conquest, but conquest should be
shaped by terrain, regional economy, population, and military realities. The
world should draw on the richness of Chinese and East Asian geography without
becoming locked into literal historical simulation.

## Decision

Use a fictional East Asia-inspired world model.

Design geography before politics. Terrain, rivers, mountains, coasts, straits,
plains, grain regions, highlands, and frontier zones are the base layer.
Factions and borders sit on top of that layer.

The world should include or allow:

- central plains and high-value agricultural basins,
- northern highlands and steppe pressure,
- western mountain passes and basin regions,
- southern grain regions and major river systems,
- eastern coastlines and northeastern plains,
- limited sea crossings inspired by straits,
- islands and maritime powers,
- a future expansion path toward northern India.

## Consequences

This makes Phase 1 primarily a terrain and regional-value redesign rather than
only a combat rebalance.

Historical geography can guide immersion and plausibility, but game balance and
readability remain valid reasons to reshape the map.

The project should avoid naming the base map in terms of political spheres
first. Use terrain and geography first, then factions.
