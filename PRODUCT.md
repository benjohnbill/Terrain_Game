# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The immediate surface is reviewed by evaluators in a school AX program through
an unaided URL. The product itself is intended for strategy players who enjoy
war simulations but avoid their long sessions, repetitive administration, or
familiar genre routines.

## Product Purpose

`Strategy Ground` presents a turn-based war strategy game in development. It
helps a first-time visitor understand that a small number of consequential
judgments about terrain, forces, information, and timing can accumulate into a
war-changing outcome. The immediate landing must communicate this without a
live presentation; the later production landing can add playable proof and
Steam-facing conversion when those assets exist.

## Positioning

A compressed war simulation where players shape the conditions of decisive
battles instead of managing a long queue of repetitive actions.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: explore the lightweight war-model interaction on the page.
- Secondary CTA: open the current development build with an explicit prototype
  label.
- Ten-second memory: strategy is not the number of orders issued, but the
  consequences created by a few connected decisions.
- Belief ladder: this is a real game in development; its product premise is
  distinct; the current page separates implemented proof from future vision;
  the underlying systems can eventually support authored war stories.
- Proof on hand: the current browser prototype, canonical design documents, and
  a map-led explanatory interaction. No sales, reviews, awards, or release-date
  claims are available.

## Brand Personality

Deliberate, consequential, and lucid. The voice is confident but not grandiose,
serious without imitating a military dashboard, and transparent about the
product's development state.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic neon command-center UI, emoji-led feature marketing, fake combat
telemetry, investor-pitch language, historical reenactment branding, and a
template grid of interchangeable feature cards. Do not imply that the current
prototype is a finished demo or that a Steam release date exists.

## Design Principles

1. Show the chain from judgment to consequence instead of listing mechanics.
2. Let geography carry the visual identity without reducing the game to terrain.
3. Separate what works now from what the product aims to become.
4. Use one meaningful interaction as evidence, not motion as decoration.
5. Make the product story understandable without narration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast, keyboard-operable controls, semantic landmarks,
visible focus states, useful text alternatives, and a reduced-motion mode. The
page must remain understandable when animation or JavaScript is unavailable.
